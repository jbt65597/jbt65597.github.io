Lemurs

By: Bailey Taylor

Lemurs are a clade of strepsirrhine primates that are native to the island of Madagascar. Due to Masagacar’s seasonal climate, lemur evolution has produced a level of diversity that is greater than any other primate group and there are over 100 species of lemurs known to mankind. Lemurs are one of my favorite animals because of the movie Madagascar where the lemurs portrayed were funny and entertaining. Modern studies of lemur ecology and behavior did not begin until the 1950s and 1960s, so research regarding lemurs. An alarming fact about lemurs is that many species are threatened with extinction due to habitat loss and hunting. I strongly believe that hunting endangered species should be a strong offense especially with beautiful creatures like the lemurs. Local efforts typically try to thwart the killing of lemurs. However, illegal logging, widespread poverty, and political instability undermine conservation efforts. The International Union of Conservation of Nature says that lemurs are the world’s most endangered mammals. In 2013 they claimed that next to 90% of all lemur species will face extinction in the next 25 years.

The graph above depicts my three favorite species of lemur. As I child I wanted a ring-tailed lemur as a pet because of its distinctive characteristics. I was enthralled by the look of its black and white tail. An interesting fact about the ring-tailed lemur is that it is high social and lives in a group of up to 30 individuals that are female dominant. My next favorite lemur is the mouse lemur. I like the mouse lemur because of its nature as a nocturnal animal and its small stature. My third favorite lemur is the silky sifaka because of its white fur and its very restricted range of only one mile of land in northeastern Madagascar.

It is truly sad that these elegant creatures are some of the most endangered animals in the entire world. In particular, the silky sifaka is one the world’s 25 most critically endangered primates as listed by the International Union for Conservation of Nature. In my honest opinion these are the list of my top three favorite lemurs:

  1. The Ring-tailed Lemur
  2. The Mouse Lemur
  3. The Silky Sifaka Lemur
